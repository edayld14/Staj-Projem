Hidiv Pavilion

Hıdiv Kasrı is a structure located on the Çubuklu hills in the Beykoz district of Istanbul. It was built in 1907 by the last Khedive of Egypt, Abbas Hilmi Pasha, designed by Italian architect Delfo Seminati in the Art Nouveau style, which was fashionable at the time.

The title "Khedive" was given to the governors of Egypt by the Ottoman Empire. Abbas Hilmi Pasha, a young Khedive of Egypt, needed to stay in Istanbul for an extended period in the late 19th century to gain support from the Ottoman Empire to counter British influence in Egypt. Consequently, in 1903, he purchased two wooden waterfront mansions on the site where the palace stands today. Abbas Hilmi Pasha soon acquired the 270-acre garden covering the wooded slopes and upper plain behind his mansions. After demolishing the wooden mansions, Abbas Hilmi Pasha had a magnificent palace built in 1907, covering 1000 square meters, along with a tower overlooking the Bosphorus, designed in the Art Nouveau style by Italian architect Delfo Seminati.

The British, who occupied Egypt, established a monarchy system in the country and stripped Abbas Hilmi Pasha of his Khedivate title. Following his dethronement, Abbas Hilmi Pasha settled (or was exiled) in Switzerland, where he lived out the rest of his life. His family stayed in the Hıdiv Kasrı until 1937, when the palace was sold to the Istanbul Municipality.
