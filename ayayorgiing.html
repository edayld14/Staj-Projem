Aya Yorgi Church

The Aya Yorgi Church is located on Büyükada in Istanbul. According to information obtained from the patriarchate records, it was built in 1751. The small church, chapel, and prayer place built at this date are known as the old church. This small, two-story, tile-roofed structure is visited by millions of tourists.

The church behind the bell tower on the hill, made of cut stone, is known as the New Aya Yorgi Church. It was built in 1905 and opened for use in 1909.

The Aya Yorgi Church, with a history spanning thousands of years, is located on Yüce Tepe at an altitude of 204 meters, one of the highest points of Büyükada. The church, named after Saint George of Cappadocia, who was martyred for his Christian faith in the 3rd century AD, is said to have been built in 963 AD during the reign of Byzantine Emperor Nikephoros Phokas.

This church, along with the House of the Virgin Mary in Ephesus, is one of the two pilgrimage sites in Turkey for Christians. Therefore, it attracts a large number of visitors every year on April 23 and September 24.

These dates are significant, of course. In the Orthodox sect, April 23 is celebrated as the "name day" of Georges. September 24 commemorates Saint Thecla, who dedicated herself to the new religion spread by Jesus' apostle Paul and was miraculously saved many times despite several attempts on her life. It is believed that those who walk to the Aya Yorgi Church barefoot and without speaking on these dates become 'semi-pilgrims.'
