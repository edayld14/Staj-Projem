Kılıç Ali Pasha Mosque

Kılıç Ali Pasha Mosque is a mosque located in the Tophane district of Istanbul, built by Mimar Sinan at the request of Kaptan-ı Derya Kılıç Ali. According to two inscriptions in the mosque, it was built in 1580 (988 AH). It also has a complex consisting of a tomb, a madrasa, and a bathhouse.

The half-domes on two sides of the dome, the arches, and the supporting walls on the other two sides make the mosque a small-scale revision of Hagia Sophia. The tiles on the mihrab side are products of İznik's brilliant period. The reason behind taking Hagia Sophia as a model is unknown. The inscriptions at the entrance, above the windows, and above the mihrab belong to Calligrapher Demircikulu Yusuf Efendi, who is considered the last representative of the Karahisari School.

Underwent significant restoration in 2011, the mosque, together with its tomb and bathhouse, is one of Mimar Sinan's valuable works, attracting both local and foreign tourists. The other sections of the complex, including the madrasa, were taken into restoration and completed in 2015. The mosque is in harmony with the Tophane Fountain, a square fountain located directly across from it, and contains many sailor tombstones. The most magnificent of these is the sarcophagus of Kaptan-ı Derya Ateş Mehmet Pasha, with a sailboat and broken mast. The mihrab protrudes from the mosque wall. There is a fountain in the entrance courtyard.

Located in Tophane Square, it is said that this mosque, built by Mimar Sinan at the request of Kaptan-ı Derya Kılıç Ali Pasha and completed in 1581, has the following story: Kılıç Ali Pasha asks Sultan Murad III for a place to build a mosque. The Sultan, due to Kılıç Ali Pasha being the Admiral of the Fleet, tells him to build a mosque on the sea. Thereupon, the Pasha agrees with Mimar Sinan and starts building the mosque by piling stone, soil, and rubble on the edge of the Tophane quay. Therefore, it is said that Kılıç Ali Pasha Mosque is the first mosque built on the sea.
