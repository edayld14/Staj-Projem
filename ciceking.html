Flower Passage

During the Tanzimat period, Sultan Abdülhamid and Sultan Abdülaziz used to visit the famous Naum Theatre located at the corner where İstiklal Avenue intersects with Sahne Street. Verdi's famous opera "II Trovatore" was staged at this theatre in Istanbul before it was performed in Paris. The Naum Theatre was destroyed by the great Beyoğlu fire in 1870. In 1876, Greek banker Hristaki Zografos Efendi built a new building on the site of the theatre. Designed by Italian architect Cleanthy Zanno, this building housed a marketplace and apartments. The lower part of the building had 24 shops arranged in Parisian style, and the upper part had 18 luxury apartments. The arcade formed by the shops was named "Hristaki Arcade," and the building was called "Cité de Pera."

In 1908, the building's ownership passed to Grand Vizier Sait Pasha, and the arcade was renamed "Sait Pasha Passage." In the 1940s, florists began to settle in the arcade, and it started to be known as "Flower Passage." Over time, bars and taverns opened, gradually displacing the florists and apartment residents. In 1988, the building was restored, and the final maintenance work was completed in 2005. The Flower Passage, with its old owners' pictures and other decorations, is one of the most ornate buildings in Beyoğlu. Can Kıraç described the enjoyable atmosphere of the Flower Passage with these words: "The Flower Passage was the first stop of Beyoğlu's evenings, almost a guiding center... Every day and night, a different world was experienced... The pleasure of daydreaming in the Flower Passage’s taverns is unique." The Flower Passage continues to serve with its beer, raki, delicious mezes, and renovated historical atmosphere.
