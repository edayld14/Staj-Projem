Malta Pavilion
Malta Pavilion is one of the two historic buildings on the east side of the wall separating Yıldız Palace from Yıldız Park in Beşiktaş, Istanbul.

It was built during the reign of Sultan Abdulaziz using stones brought from Malta, hence the name. It has been the site of significant historical events. After the unsuccessful attempt to enthrone Sultan V. Murat, he was held in the pavilion for security reasons, and Mithat Pasha was tried in a tent set up on the flat area behind the pavilion. The ceiling decorations, marble fish patterns, and the gilded mirror are worth seeing.

Tent Pavilion
In 1871, the pavilion, known as the "Pavilion of the Sedir" was built in the grove belonging to Çırağan Palace at the request of Sultan Abdulaziz (1861–1876). The garden decoration was done by Sarkis Balyan and his brothers. After Sultan II. Abdülhamid (1876–1909) came to the throne, he found living by the sea dangerous and moved to Yıldız Palace, incorporating some parts of Çırağan Palace into this palace. The Tent Pavilion was used for the interrogation of Mithat Pasha and his associates, and Mithat Pasha was held in the pavilion's basement for sixty-six days. After these events, the pavilion was closed and used only for a few hours during harem visits.

After Abdulhamid’s departure, the pavilion remained closed for many years. In 1940, it was transferred to the Istanbul Metropolitan Municipality by the Ministry of Finance and, after the grove was named "Yıldız Park," the Tent Pavilion was repaired. Between 1949-1960, it was operated as “Markiz Patisserie” by a pastry shop owner named Avadis Çakır. After the May 27, 1960 Coup, the Tent Pavilion was turned into the Tanzimat Museum. In 1982, the rights for use and operation, along with other pavilions, were given to the Turkish Touring and Automobile Club.

According to the "Maintenance Item" of the High Council for the Protection of Cultural and Natural Assets’ decision dated February 28, 1995, and numbered 378, restoration work began in June 1995 when the pavilion was transferred to the Istanbul Metropolitan Municipality. The pavilion was fully restored and has been operated as a restaurant by Beltur since January 1, 1997.
