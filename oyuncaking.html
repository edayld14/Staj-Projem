Oyuncak Museum

The Istanbul Toy Museum is a toy museum located in the Göztepe district of Istanbul, established in 2005 by poet Sunay Akın.

The collection consists of antique toys symbolizing different cultures, purchased by Sunay Akın over 20 years from more than 40 countries for the purpose of creating a museum. The museum, which opened on April 23, 2005, has served as an example for toy museums opened in other cities in Turkey.

After visiting the toy museum in Nuremberg, Germany, poet Sunay Akın conducted research on the history of toys and spent 20 years purchasing toys from collectors to establish a toy museum in Turkey. In 2005, he founded the museum in a historic mansion inherited from his family in the Göztepe district of Istanbul.

Opened on April 23, 2005, the museum is the second toy museum in Turkey, following the museum opened at Ankara University in 1990. The museum's management was taken over by Sunay Akın's wife, philosophy teacher Belgin Akın. The design of the museum was done by stage designer Ayhan Doğan.

In 2012, the Istanbul Toy Museum was selected by the European Museum Academy as one of the 13 best toy museums in Europe. In November of the same year, it hosted the first meeting of the Toy and Children's Museums Association (TOYCO), founded by Sunay Akın to bring together similar museums from around the world.

The museum has served as a model for the establishment of new toy museums in Antalya, Ataşehir, and Gaziantep, with the consultancy and curation of Sunay Akın.

The story of the museum is also the subject of a book titled "A Toy Museum Story" (2014), which includes the life story and toy memories of retired pilot Gürol Kutlu, who has been volunteering at the museum since 2005.

In 2023, the Istanbul Toy Museum held a year-long exhibition titled "Turkish Toys" at the Nuremberg Toy Museum, which inspired the establishment of the museum.
