Ortakoy Mosque

The Büyük Mecidiye Mosque, or Ortaköy Mosque, is a Neo-Baroque style mosque located on the shores of Ortaköy in the Beşiktaş district of Istanbul.

The mosque was commissioned by Sultan Abdülmecid and built in 1853 by the Armenian architect Nigoğos Balyan. Like all imperial mosques, it consists of two sections: the prayer hall and the sultan's section. The wide and tall windows are arranged to bring the changing light of the Bosphorus into the mosque.

The mosque has two minarets with a single balcony each, accessed by stairs. The walls are made of white cut stone. The walls of the single dome are adorned with pink mosaics. The mihrab is made of mosaic and marble, while the minbar is covered with marmalade and is a product of fine craftsmanship.

Being quite delicate from a structural perspective, the mosque was repaired in 1862 and 1866. After suffering significant damage in the 1894 earthquake, it was fully repaired by the Directorate of Pious Foundations (Evkaf Nezâreti) in 1909. During this restoration, the old fluted minarets that had collapsed were rebuilt without fluting, and the sections of the minarets, as well as various parts of the mosque, were renewed. In the 1960s, due to new cracks in the building, the General Directorate of Foundations initiated restoration work, reinforcing the foundation and renewing the dome. The mosque, which had been closed for worship during this repair, was reopened in 1969. After suffering partial damage from a major fire in 1984, the building was restored again.

Restoration work conducted by the General Directorate of Foundations between 2011 and 2014 took approximately three years and was completed on June 6, 2014. The mosque was reopened for worship and visitation with a ceremony attended by the then Prime Minister and current President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan.
