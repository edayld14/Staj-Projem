St. Anthony Church

St. Anthony Catholic Church is the largest and most populous Catholic church in Istanbul. It is located on İstiklal Street in Beyoğlu, on the left side as you go from Galatasaray to Tünel.

In 1230, priests built a church in the Galata area in honor of their founder, St. Francis of Assisi. The Church of St. Francis, which was burned twice in the fires of 1639 and 1660 and rebuilt in the same place each time, was relocated to its current location in Beyoğlu after the fire of 1696, which destroyed the entire surrounding area. In 1724, a new church named St. Anthony was built and consecrated in Pera for the Catholic citizens and their families who served in the Ottoman Imperial Palace and State services and were also engaged in trade.

The current church, with a façade made of red brick, was started in 1906 on the site of the old church and was completed in 1912. On February 15th, the anniversary of the transfer of St. Anthony’s remains to the Basilica of Padua, the priests moved into their new church, and it was consecrated and opened for worship. It was built in the Italian Neo-Gothic style by the Italian architect Giulio Mongeri, using reinforced concrete. It measures 20x50 meters and is constructed in the Latin cross form and Neo-Gothic style. The church walls are covered with mosaic up to a certain height, and the external walls of the structure are made of brick. The entrance to the church is through a gate between two buildings constructed to provide income to the church. This gate opens into the church's courtyard, and the width of this façade facing İstiklal Street is 38 meters. The church is managed by Italian priests.

The two six-story apartment buildings, connected by a passage, in front of the courtyard entrance on İstiklal Street were built to generate income for the church. These are the St. Antoine Apartments and are among the first reinforced concrete structures on İstiklal Street.
