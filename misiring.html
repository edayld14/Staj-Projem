Spice Bazaar

The Spice Bazaar is located behind the New Mosque in Eminönü, next to the Flower Bazaar. It is one of Istanbul's oldest covered bazaars. Famous for its herbalists, the bazaar still sells products in line with its old traditions, such as natural medicines, spices, flower seeds, and rare plant roots and barks, as well as nuts, delicatessen products, and various food items. The Spice Bazaar is also open on Sundays.

History
It is said that during the Byzantine era, there was a bazaar called Makro Envalos at the same location. The current building was constructed in 1660 by Turhan Sultan and the chief architect Kâzım Ağa. Initially known as the New Bazaar or the Valide Bazaar, and according to legend, built with taxes collected from Egypt, it began to be referred to by its present name after the 18th century. It survived two major fire threats in 1691 and 1940. The bazaar was last restored by the Istanbul Municipality between 1940 and 1943.

Architecture
The L-shaped structure adjacent to the New Mosque has an arched entrance. One of these is the Haseki Gate. The upper part of this entrance is two stories high, with the upper floor having once served as a court where tradesmen and the public could resolve their issues.
