Yıldız Park

During the Ottoman period, especially in the early 1600s, it started to gain prominence. The land, formerly known as Kazancıoğlu Gardens and owned by this family, was purchased by Sultan Murad IV and gifted to his daughter Kaya Sultan. During the Tulip Era, the gardens served as a venue for various entertainments during the Çırağan festivals, which were organized based on decorative tastes. Initially part of the Çırağan Palace’s rear garden, it later became the greenery of the outer courtyard of the Yıldız Palace, which was expanded from 1877. After 1940, it was named Yıldız Park.

In 1925, the Şale Pavilion, which was given to an Italian operator and used as a casino, was taken from the operator through the intervention of Atatürk. In the 1930s, it was divided into three parts; the woodland on the sea side with Çadır and Malta Pavilions was given to the Istanbul Municipality, the structures on the hilltop were given to the War Academy (transferred to the Ministry of Culture in 1978), and the Şale Pavilion on the northern side of the wall was given to the Turkish Grand National Assembly.

In 1979, a contract between the Turkish Touring and Automobile Association (TTOK) and the Istanbul Municipality entrusted the maintenance of Yıldız Park, along with the restoration of the Malta and Çadır Pavilions, to this organization for 15 years. Following the expiration of the agreement in 1994, the usage rights were returned to the Istanbul Metropolitan Municipality.
