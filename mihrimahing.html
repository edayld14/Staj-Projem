Mihrimah Sultan Mosque

Mihrimah Sultan Mosque, located at Üsküdar pier square, is the mosque built by Mimar Sinan for Mihrimah Sultan, the daughter of Sultan Suleiman the Magnificent. It is also one of Mimar Sinan's early works. The dome is supported by half-domes on three sides, but there is no half-dome on the front façade.

In April and May, from the Bayezid fire tower or a high point in that area looking towards the İskele Mosque; at sunrise, the rising sun can be seen between the two minarets of the İskele Mosque, and at sunset (on the 14th of each lunar month), the rising moon can be observed. Looking towards the western horizon from the same tower towards Edirnekapı, the moon can be seen rising in the morning and the sun setting in the evening at Mihr-i Mah Sultan Edirnekapı Complex. Hence, Mihr-i Mah means Sun and Moon.

ABOUT THE MOSQUE
The mosque’s dome has a diameter of ten meters. Its two single-balconied minarets, muqarnas mihrab, and marble minbar reflect the strongest forms of classical architecture. The mosque is one of the prominent examples of architecture on the Anatolian side and carries traces of the past. On the side facing the sea, there is a twenty-sided marble ablution fountain that gives the building a distinct aesthetic appearance with the arcade surrounding the front courtyard.

The mosque's courtyard is smaller at the front compared to other historical mosques. There is a spacious area on the right side and the qibla wall. When viewed from the pier, the mosque resembles an eagle silhouette. The fountain side of the courtyard has been added to the front courtyard and designed to protect against the sea breeze.
